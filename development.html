Revitalise, Don't Overdevelop:
Promoting Sustainable Living
By Renovating Old Houses

Welcome to our web page dedicated to putting an end to overdevelopmet in Malta, where there are
constantly new buildings being constructed. The ongoing constructions cause not only noise but also visual
pollution to passerbys. In order to obtain a more sustainable community, we need to restore what we laready
have: old buildings instead of building new ones. Revive, renew, redefine!

Internal Page - Content Structure

Due to Malta's growing population, there are constantly new properties being built. This has raised significant concers as the new construction sites polluted the area. It has also overshadowed Malta's natural beauty and its buildings with traditional Maltese architechture. This has caused a great loss of identity for Maltese people. These traditional buildings that used to be timeless are now rotting as they are either abandoned, vacant or not taken care of.

"While these 81,613 residential properties are empty or underutilized, the building industry continues to build more, and the problem continues to grow. They call this progress, but it is in fact environmental destruction of the little land we have to build property that is then left empty or underutilised."- Independent Newspaper
