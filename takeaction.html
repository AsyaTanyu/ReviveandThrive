Revitalise, Don't Overdevelop:
Promoting Sustainable Living
By Renovating Old Houses

Welcome to our web page dedicated to putting an end to overdevelopmet in Malta, where there are
constantly new buildings being constructed. The ongoing constructions cause not only noise but also visual
pollution to passerbys. In order to obtain a more sustainable community, we need to restore what we laready
have: old buildings instead of building new ones. Revive, renew, redefine!

The government has made efforts such as giving incentives and paying some of the renovation bills for traditional Maltese houses in order to encourage more buyers to invest in restoring these historic properties. The government will refund up to €54,000 on the first €300,000 spent on renovation work for certain properties. This is eligible for properties over 20 years old that have been vacant for more than 7 years.Unfortunately, buyers are often not aware of this fact and think that renovating a house costs too much money and time. Therefore, they end up buying a recently built house. Now that you know this, consider investing in a traditional Maltese house and see below for some already renovated Maltese homes for inspiration. Renovations made simple, results made extraordinary!
